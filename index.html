About Me

I joined the University of Illinois at Chicago as an assistant professor of Business Analytics in Fall 2020. I received my Ph.D in Operations Research from MIT in Spring 2020, supervised by Dimitris Bertsimas. Prior to MIT, I completed my undergraduate studies at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign in Computer Engineering and spent time at Google and Facebook.

My research focuses on the intersection of optimization under uncertainty and machine learning, with applications to operations, business analytics, and finance. I am particularly interested in the development of tractable algorithms for stochastic dynamic optimization problems in data-driven environments.

During graduate school, I received the MIT Sloan Outstanding Teaching Assistant Award (selected once per year by MBA students) and second place in the INFORMS George Nicholson Student Paper Competition. I also organized the student-run January class “15.S60 Computing for Statistics and Optimization” in 2017, 2018, and 2019.

More information on my work can be found on my research page and Google Scholar.
