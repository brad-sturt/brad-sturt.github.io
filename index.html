About Me

Welcome to my website! I am an assistant professor of Business Analytics at the University of Illinois at Chicago. I previously received my Ph.D in Operations Research from MIT in Spring 2020, where I was supervised by Dimitris Bertsimas. Prior to MIT, I completed my undergraduate studies at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign in Computer Engineering.

My research focuses on optimization under uncertainty and machine learning in the context of operations management, finance, and business analytics. I am particularly interested in the development of tractable methods for solving stochastic and robust optimization problems that arise in managerial and data-driven applications.

I am passionate about teaching! At UIC, I mainly teach MBA and undergraduate classes on business analytics and statistcs. During graduate school, I received the MIT Sloan Outstanding Teaching Assistant Award (selected once per year by MBA students) and co-organized the student-run class “15.S60 Computing for Statistics and Optimization” in 2017, 2018, and 2019.

More information about me can be found on my research page, my CV, and Google Scholar.
